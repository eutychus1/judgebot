Case 2: School Proctoring AI

AI flags students as “cheating” based on eye movement but mislabels neurodivergent students.

My Inspector’s Take — What’s Happening

This AI is a digital hall monitor. It assumes “normal” exam behavior is stillness, but neurodivergent students naturally move differently, causing unfair flags.

My Top 5 Moves to Fix It

  1. Validate across diverse groups: Test on neurodiverse students to reduce false positives.
  2. Less invasive data: Track only minimal signals, not eye movements 24/7.
  3. Alternative assessment paths: Provide non-AI proctoring or accommodations.
  4. Explainable flags: Show why a student was flagged for easier review.
  5. Oversight committee: Create a board to audit fairness and privacy regularly.

I imagine myself sneaking into the virtual exam hall whispering to the AI: “Stop treating every blink like a crime.” Serious fairness with a touch of humor keeps us human.
